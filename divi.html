Section 1: Fundamental Division Concepts

1.1 Understanding Division

Division is the process of splitting a number into equal parts:

a ÷ b = c

Where:

  • a is the dividend (total quantity)
  • b is the divisor (number of groups)
  • c is the quotient (size of each group)

Division as Repeated Subtraction

How many times can you subtract 4 from 12?

12 - 4 = 8 (1 time)

8 - 4 = 4 (2 times)

4 - 4 = 0 (3 times)

12 ÷ 4 = 3

Section 2: Division Methods

2.1 Short Division (Bus Stop Method)

For dividing by single-digit divisors:

Steps for Short Division
  1. Write the dividend under the division symbol
  2. Divide each digit from left to right
  3. Carry over remainders to the next digit
Example

Calculate 486 ÷ 3:

     1 6 2
   --------
3 ) 4 8 6
   - 3
   ----
     1 8
     - 1 8
     ----
       0 6
       - 6
       ----
         0

486 ÷ 3 = 162

2.2 Long Division

For dividing by multi-digit divisors:

Long Division Steps
  1. Divide: How many times does the divisor fit into the first part?
  2. Multiply: Multiply the divisor by your answer
  3. Subtract: Subtract from the dividend
  4. Bring down: Bring down the next digit
  5. Repeat until no digits remain
Example

Calculate 1,536 ÷ 24:

       64
    --------
24 ) 1536
     -144
     -----
        96
       -96
       -----
         0

24 × 6 = 144, subtract from 153 → remainder 9

Bring down 6 → 96

24 × 4 = 96, subtract → remainder 0

1,536 ÷ 24 = 64

Section 3: Advanced Division Concepts

3.1 Remainders and Fractions

When division isn't exact, we can express the result in different ways:

Remainder Form

17 ÷ 5 = 3 R2

Fraction Form

17 ÷ 5 = 3 2/5

Decimal Form

17 ÷ 5 = 3.4

3.2 Dividing Decimals

Decimal Division Method
  1. Move the decimal point in the divisor to make it a whole number
  2. Move the decimal point in the dividend the same number of places
  3. Divide as usual, placing the decimal point in the quotient above
Example

Calculate 4.5 ÷ 0.15:

Move decimal 2 places: 450 ÷ 15

       30
    --------
15 ) 450
     -45
     -----
        00
       - 0
       -----
         0

4.5 ÷ 0.15 = 30

Section 4: Real-World Applications

4.1 Word Problems

Problem 1

A school needs to transport 240 students on buses that each hold 36 students. How many buses are needed?

240 ÷ 36 ≈ 6.666 → Need 7 buses (can't have part of a bus)

Problem 2

If 5.5 pounds of apples cost $8.25, what's the price per pound?

$8.25 ÷ 5.5 = $1.50 per pound
